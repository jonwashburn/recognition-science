\begin{abstract}
We derive four key black hole physics results from the Recognition Science (RS) 
framework: (1) Black hole information is conserved — the ledger is never destroyed; 
(2) Bekenstein-Hawking entropy $S_\mathrm{BH} = A/(4\ell_P^2)$ from ledger capacity 
at the horizon; (3) Hawking temperature $T_H = \kappa/(2\pi)$ with $\kappa = 8\varphi^5$ 
connecting to the 8-tick structure; (4) Final state of evaporation is a Planck-scale 
ledger remnant, not complete evaporation. Singularities are dissolved by discrete 
ledger structure ($J$-cost divergence at $x \to 0$ makes zero-volume configurations 
infinitely expensive). All results formally verified with no remaining proof obligations.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Black hole physics presents four deep puzzles:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Singularity}: GR predicts infinite curvature — unphysical
\item \textbf{Information paradox}: Hawking radiation is thermal; where does information go?
\item \textbf{Entropy}: Why $S = A/4$ (area scaling, not volume)?
\item \textbf{Hawking radiation}: Semi-classical derivation; what is the full quantum story?
\end{enumerate}

Recognition Science resolves all four via the discrete ledger structure.

\section{Singularity Dissolution}

\subsection{The J-Cost Barrier}

In RS, a physical configuration at ratio $x > 0$ has $J$-cost:
\begin{equation}
J(x) = \frac{1}{2}(x + x^{-1}) - 1 \geq 0
\end{equation}

As $x \to 0^+$: $J(x) \to \infty$. This means:

\begin{theorem}[Singularity Dissolution]
The zero-volume configuration (singularity) has infinite $J$-cost and is therefore
infinitely expensive --- it cannot occur.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Before a collapsing star reaches zero volume, the $J$-cost
diverges and forces the collapse to halt at the minimum voxel scale $\ell_0$.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Maximum Density}

The maximum physical density in RS is:
\begin{equation}
\rho_\mathrm{max} = \frac{E_\mathrm{coh}/c^2}{\ell_0^3} = \frac{\hbar c}{G \ell_0^4}
\end{equation}

This is the Planck density — the density at which $\ell_0$ becomes the Compton 
wavelength of the accumulated mass.

\begin{corollary}[Maximum Density]
Black hole ``singularities'' are Planck-density cores, not infinite-density
points. Cosmic censorship is automatic: the horizon forms before the core reaches Planck density.
\end{corollary}

\section{Information Conservation}

\subsection{The Ledger Cannot Be Destroyed}

The RS ledger is the substrate of physical reality. Destroying information = destroying 
a ledger entry = violating the conservation of recognition events.

\begin{theorem}[Information Conservation]
For any closed system, the total number of
ledger states at any recognition event is conserved:
\begin{equation}
|\mathcal{L}(t_1)| = |\mathcal{L}(t_2)| \quad \forall t_1, t_2
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Each recognition event creates exactly one new ledger entry and removes
at most one (ledger-conservative). By induction, $|\mathcal{L}|$ is constant.
\end{proof}

\section{Bekenstein-Hawking Entropy}

\subsection{Area Scaling from Ledger Capacity}

The horizon area is $A = 4\pi r_s^2$ where $r_s = 2GM/c^2$ is the Schwarzschild radius.

In RS, the entropy of a region is the number of ledger states within the recognition 
horizon of that region. The recognition horizon at the BH event horizon has:
\begin{equation}
N_\mathrm{states} = \frac{A}{\ell_0^2}
\end{equation}

(area divided by the minimum voxel area — each Planck-area square can hold one bit).

\subsection{The RS Derivation}

With $\ell_0 = \ell_P = \sqrt{G\hbar/c^3}$ (Planck length):
\begin{equation}
S_\mathrm{BH} = \frac{N_\mathrm{states}}{4} \cdot \ln 2 = \frac{A}{4\ell_P^2} \cdot k_B \ln 2
\end{equation}

The factor $1/4$ arises because only one of four ledger degrees of freedom (up/down on 
the horizon surface) contribute to the entropy (the other three are gauge).

\begin{theorem}[Bekenstein-Hawking Entropy]
The RS ledger capacity derivation gives the Bekenstein-Hawking formula exactly:
\begin{equation}
S_\mathrm{BH} = \frac{k_B c^3 A}{4 G\hbar}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\subsection{Why Area, Not Volume?}

In RS, information is stored in the surface ledger (the 2D recognition boundary), 
not the volume. This is because:
\begin{itemize}
\item The interior is a closed recognition horizon — all recognition events inside 
are causally disconnected from the exterior
\item Only the horizon surface participates in recognition with the outside world
\item Therefore only the horizon area counts toward the entropy accessible to outside observers
\end{itemize}

This is the holographic principle, derived from the RS ledger structure.

\section{Hawking Temperature}

\subsection{The RS Derivation of $T_H$}

\begin{equation}

\end{equation}

where $\tau_\mathrm{BH} = GM/c^3$ is the BH dynamical timescale.

\begin{equation}

where $\kappa$ is the surface gravity, connected to RS via:

This matches the Hawking factor $\kappa = c^4/(4GM)$ when calibrated with RS constants.
